Borani Banjan

Ingredients:

  • 3 medium eggplants
  • 2 cups strained yogurt
  • 2 cloves garlic, crushed
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Peel the eggplants and cut them into medium slices.
  2. Fry the eggplants in oil until golden and tender.
  3. Mix the crushed garlic with yogurt, salt, and pepper.
  4. Arrange the fried eggplants on a plate and cover with the garlic yogurt.
  5. Garnish with dried mint or chili if desired and serve.

Mantu

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 200 g ground meat
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 tbsp chopped cilantro
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Yogurt and dried mint for garnish

Instructions:

  1. Mix flour with water and a pinch of salt to make a soft dough.
  2. Combine ground meat, grated onion, cilantro, salt, and pepper.
  3. Roll out the dough and cut into small squares.
  4. Place some meat filling in each square and seal well.
  5. Steam the mantoo and serve with yogurt and mint on top.

Kabab

Ingredients:

  • 500g lamb or beef
  • 1 large onion, grated
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • 1 teaspoon cumin (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Cut the meat into small pieces suitable for skewering.
  2. Mix the meat with grated onion, salt, pepper, and cumin.
  3. Marinate for at least one hour.
  4. Thread the meat onto skewers and grill over charcoal until cooked through and golden.
  5. Serve hot with fresh bread, herbs, and yogurt drink.

Shola-e Mash

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup rice
  • ¬Ω cup mung beans
  • 1 medium onion
  • 2 tablespoons oil
  • Salt and spices (turmeric, pepper) to taste
  • 2 cups water

Instructions:

  1. Wash the mung beans and cook them halfway in water.
  2. Chop the onion and fry it in oil until golden brown.
  3. Add the washed rice to the mung beans.
  4. Add the fried onion, salt, and spices. Let everything cook together.
  5. When the rice and mung beans are completely tender, serve hot and enjoy!

Jalebi

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon yeast
  • 1 cup lukewarm water
  • 2 cups sugar
  • ¬Ω cup water (for syrup)
  • A bit of saffron or rose water
  • Oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Mix the flour, yeast, and lukewarm water to make a smooth batter. Let it rest for about an hour to rise.
  2. Boil sugar and water to make syrup and add saffron or rose water for flavor.
  3. Pour the batter into a piping bag and fry in hot oil, shaping spirals until golden.
  4. Dip the fried jalebi immediately into the warm syrup and serve fresh.
